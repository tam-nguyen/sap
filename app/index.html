Berlin: A Taste of the Past and the Present

17-19 November 2018 Hosted by Julie and Bill McDermott

Welcome to Berlin!

Berlin is, without a doubt, one of Europe’s most-renowned cities, and for very good reason. From its youthful energy and ground-breaking tech scene to its historical architecture and political prestige, this is a city that brims with innovation and character.

As part of this social programme, we will explore vestiges of Berlin’s layered past whilst also discovering the promise of Germany’s future. It is celebrated for its engineering feats, its spectacular museums and its magnificent World Heritage Sites, not to mention its world-class culinary scene, arts and culture. From an exciting events calendar to a rich tapestry of pre and post-war architecture, Berlin’s heritage leaves a lasting impression on everyone who spends time here. And attracting close to a million visitors each year, the world-famous Berlin Wall has been dubbed the longest open-air gallery in the world.

Berlin’s past has of course, at times, been dark and complex. This vast metropolis comprises some intriguing and varied layers and has been imaginatively reborn over recent years. The Golden Twenties in particular, was a particularly colourful era for the city. It was during this interwar period, dubbed The Weimar Republic, when Germany’s culture and literature was flourishing. Berliners lived life to the full and the city saw an injection of politically provocative arts, as well as a boom in pioneering work across science and literature. This convivial era, which coincided with hedonistic parties and political freedom, lasted until the inception of Nazi rule and is looked back on with great nostalgia. Today, remnants of Weimar culture can still be glimpsed across the city in the way of retro movie theatres, quirky jazz bars and iconic art institutions. Important monuments which symbolise Berlin’s more promising future following the fall of the Berlin Wall, can also be discovered across the city. The Brandenburg Gate is of course, one of the most famous, and celebrates peace and unity for the country.

The creative spirit is still palpable in Berlin and it now boasts one of the fastest-growing start-up scenes in the world. Offering a true taste of this entrepreneurial culture, Silicon Allee, is located in the heart of Berlin Mitte and is an ‘embassy’ to the city’s thriving start-up scene. Playing home to some of Europe’s most successful innovators, Silicon Allee uncovers inspiring co-working and co-living spaces, exciting independent businesses and a diverse community of tech entrepreneurs. This thriving campus also hosts leading digital conferences and events and is well and truly leading the way for the digital age.

If your heart leads with your taste buds, Berlin will win you over with its exciting culinary scene. No longer limited to the traditional bratwurst and bier, Berlin’s gastronomy has earned prestige amongst food critics from all over the world. From artisanal start-ups to Michelin Star-worthy dining, it is fast becoming a world-class food and drink destination.

We are delighted to host you for this special event and we think you will agree, Berlin is a special city. Your social programme at the ERT has been carefully designed to help you enjoy a world-class experience of award-winning chefs, exquisite hospitality, top influential speakers and unmissable sights and attractions. We look forward to showing you a sample of the very best of Germany’s capital city and hope you have a truly memorable and inspiring time together.
